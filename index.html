What "Home" Means to Me

My family means the world to me, and I was raised very close with my parents and three brothers. I am the second oldest, at age 20. My older brother Andrew is 24 and my younger brothers Nicholas and Luke are 18 and 16 years old. I treasure the time that I spend with my brothers, and even though two of us are no longer living in our childhood home because we are older, we make a huge effort to find a way to spend time there all together. While the pandemic has been hard for everyone in the world, one blessing that came out of it is that my family was able to spend a lot of time as a whole with all 6 of us. School was online and so was work, so we all spent our days on our computers and our nights laughing around the kitchen table. This is time that none of us ever thought we would get to have in our home all together again and that none of us will ever forget. My home is wherever they are. Whether that is in the suburbs of Pittsburgh or at a rental house in Topsail Island, North Carolina in the summers (our favorite place to vacation to), it does not matter where we are so long as we are together.

Imaginary App

Don't Break the Bank is a digital piggy bank that you set your money into before going out. It links to your bank account information and allows you to budget a certain amount and then prevents you from making poor financial choices after that set amount is used up.

PDF of my Imaginary App example.

I came up with this IA idea when we were brainstorming in class about different ideas for apps that intersect the typical thought process behind doing typical tasks. When you go out with friends, it is a subconscious act to take your card out and pay without thinking about the amount left in your bank account. During my discussion with classmates we also talked about how we have heard many stories of people going out with friends and waking up the next morning with a much greater amount gone from their bank account than they expected. This is what jumpstarted this idea in my brain for a virtual piggy bank which locks in a maximum amount you set for yourself to spend before going out. I created my image in InDesign to depict someone holding their phone with the app open to the virtual piggy bank image and the depiction of money spent versus money left. I believe that this app would impact the way that people budget for personal or social areas, especially surrounding going out for food and drinks with friends or going on trips or vacations. This is the goal I had in mind so that there would be an in between in people's thought processes before completing a transaction through the app. I got stuck in thinking about how this would come into fruition/regulations with banks, but since this is all hypothetical I think it is a good start to a plan.
